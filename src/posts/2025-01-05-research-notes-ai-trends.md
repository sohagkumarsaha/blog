## Why these notes matter

AI tools are becoming part of everyday research practice. The important question is not whether researchers should use AI, but how to use it responsibly, reproducibly, and critically.

## Trends I am watching

- **Small domain models:** Specialized tools may outperform general tools when the task is narrow and the data are structured.
- **Retrieval-augmented workflows:** Search plus generation is useful for literature notes, lab documentation, and internal knowledge bases.
- **Human verification loops:** AI outputs are most useful when paired with careful human review and testable evidence.
- **Code-aware assistants:** Programming support is improving, especially for debugging, refactoring, and explaining legacy scripts.

## Practical research uses

1. Summarizing long technical documents into review notes.
2. Drafting outlines for papers, reports, and lectures.
3. Generating small code examples for teaching.
4. Checking consistency between claims, figures, and references.

## Caution

AI should support scholarship, not replace judgment. Every generated claim, equation, citation, and code result should be checked before it becomes part of academic work.
